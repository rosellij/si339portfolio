Venice is also at the top of my list of places to travel to. Yes, it's a really popular place to go, and a bit cliche, but it's no myth that Venice is, in fact, sinking in addition to the threats it faces from this planet's rising sea level. Personally, I just want to see it before I'm no longer able to.

Regardless of all that, it does seem like a place with no equal, in a similar vain to Singapore, but for different reasons. You get around everywhere by canal and walkways! I just think that's neat, you know? There's also all the wonderful Renaissance architecture, little alleys to get lost amongst, and there's also gotta be some wonderful food to enjoy, right? I did a little reading and found out that Venetian food is a little different from that of the rest of Italy, that their whole shtick is using squid ink ("nella seppia") to flavor and color their food. The city's also home to my favorite dessert, Tiramisu, which, opposed to other Italian favorites such as Pasta alla Carbonara, has only been around since the sixties, for a handful of decades.

Here's a blurb about Venice from our friends at Wikivoyage: Venice is a sanctuary on a lagoon that is virtually the same as it was 600 years ago, which adds to the fascinating character. Venice has decayed since its heyday and suffers from overtourism, but the romantic charm remains. It is also known as the birthplace of composers Tomaso Albinoni and Antonio Vivaldi. Venice and its lagoon are a UNESCO World Heritage site. It used to be an independent republic, and remains one of Italy's most important cities, with a quarter million inhabitants.
