These first three places are, of course, only a small sampling of all the ones I wish to visit. On this page, I've included a few more places that are high on my list but didn't make the cut to get an entire page about them. Many of these places are also so high up on my list simply because, due to rapidly changing social, political, and environmental factors, they may not be what they are today in only a couple decades. Nonetheless, they're all places that seem super interesting to me, and I very much still want to visit each and every one of them as soon as I'm able.

Regardless of whether or not you feel similarly about any of the places on my particular list, I encourage you to think yourself about those places that you want to visit, for whatever reason, even if it may be out of reach at the moment. Dreams are what keep us going as people, and having such a clear one as getting to a physical place can be an especially potent one! Do your best to use these dreams simply as goals, though, not as an escape from your own life. Do feel strongly about these dreams, but not so strongly that they overtake who you are and what you are doing right now. Today, do what you are able to, and tomorrow, you might just be a step or two closer to reaching that place of yours!.

Thanks for visiting my site!


Tibet and Nepal

While physical landscape of Tibet will most certaintly change dreastically in the coming years, its culture is changing even faster: the Beijing government is systematically attempting to replace the Tibetan people and their traditions with Han Chinese stand-ins. I hope to get a glimpse of this mile-high country, its resilient people and their way of life before they are no more.


Sierra Madre Occidental, Mexico

While the Sierra Madre Occidental, like most of Mexico, have a constantly in-flux level of danger for travelers, it's one of the largest regions in North America I've found of my favorite climate type: CSB. I just think it'd be neat to go camping for a while somewhere like that, where the weather is not too cool or too warm, away from big cities and people.


Glacier National Park, Montana

Glacier National Park is another wonderful destination on my list that's accessible by train, and another one of those unique ecosystems currently undergoing radical changes at the hands of humans. Just look at those little flowers! Everything looks so big there! I've never adventured in the Rocky Mountains before at any rate.


West Coast, United States

Yes, this location is a little bit vague, but I'm hoping to stop in several places, only for a bit, as I travel it from South to North! A treasure trove of uniquely North American sceneries and cities, I'm looking forward to trying all the awesome food and enjoying the nice weather, at least for the first bit, and taking in this region of the US while it's still in its prime.


New Orleans, Louisiana

This city is also famous for how it's sinking, although it doesn't have as many canals as Venice. The city makes up for what it lacks in municipal waterways in its completely unique vibe, culture, food, music, et cetera. This city is one of the most culturally important in the United States' history, yet it is also unfortunately one of its most hurricane-prone.


Sao Luis
and the Amazon Rainforest, Brazil

The entire city of Sao Luis, located at the mouth of the Mearim river, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is over 400 years old. It's home to tons of old Portuguese architecture and is a key gateway to the Brazilian interior. Looking forward to doing something I've never done before: traveling by riverboat deep into an unknown land!


The Countries of the Persian Gulf

Most all the countries around the Persian Gulf are currently undergoing a boom, one of the biggest moments for Southwest Asia since the Islamic Golden Age. I've never been over there, but I very much would like to take a peek at the incredible phenomenon of the rise of countries like Qatar and the UAE before this miracle fizzles out.



Alaska, United States

Alaska has always held a special place in my heart since I was young. I visited briefly when I was younger and thought I was going to live there! If it weren't so cold and rainy, the stunning landscapes and excellent seafood might be worth putting up with the remoteness.



Patagonia, Argentina

The Alaska of South America! An equally legendary region, home to whoknowswhat and some of the closest land to Antarctica. I heard about a chef dude who lives out there and cooks awesome meals for people in his remote cabin. Maybe I can find him there, and I can learn some life lessons about living in such a place?
